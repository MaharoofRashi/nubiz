## PAGE 1: DUBAI MAINLAND
**URL:** /business-setup/mainland
**Tagline:** Trade Freely Across the UAE

### What Is Dubai Mainland?
A mainland company is licensed by Dubai's Department of Economy and Tourism (DET) — the authority that replaced the old DED in 2021 when Dubai merged its economy and tourism departments. A DET license gives you the right to trade anywhere in the UAE without restriction — with any customer, in any location, across all seven emirates.

This is the fundamental difference from a freezone company. Mainland = no geographic or commercial restrictions on who you can sell to in the UAE.

### The Big Change Since 2021
Before June 2021, mainland companies in most sectors required a UAE national to hold 51% of the shares — the infamous "local sponsor" requirement. The 2021 amendments to the Commercial Companies Law changed this. Most commercial and industrial activities now allow 100% foreign ownership on the mainland. The old 51% rule is effectively gone for the vast majority of business types.

Some restricted activities still require a UAE national partner or a Local Service Agent (LSA) — but these are specific strategic sectors (defence, certain professional activities), not the norm for general business.

### Key Characteristics
- **Licensing authority:** Dubai DET (Department of Economy and Tourism)
- **Market access:** Unrestricted — any UAE customer, any location, government contracts
- **Ownership:** 100% foreign ownership for most activities (since 2021)
- **Office requirement:** Physical office with registered Ejari (tenancy agreement) is mandatory
- **Minimum space:** 200 square feet for most professional and commercial licenses
- **Visa quota:** ~1 visa per 80 sq ft of office space — scalable with office size, no hard upper limit
- **Setup time:** Typically 10-15 working days
- **Corporate tax:** Standard 9% on profits above AED 375,000

### Why Choose Mainland

**1. Unrestricted UAE market access**
Mainland companies can sell directly to any UAE customer — consumers, other businesses, government entities — without needing a distributor or intermediary. If your customers are in the UAE, mainland is the clean answer.

**2. Government tenders and contracts**
Only mainland-licensed companies can bid for UAE federal and emirate government contracts directly. This is a major revenue stream in construction, IT services, consulting, facilities management, healthcare, education, and many other sectors. Freezone companies are excluded from most government procurement.

**3. No restrictions on hiring or office location**
Mainland companies can set up offices anywhere in Dubai (subject to zoning). Visa quotas scale with office size — there's no fixed upper limit. As you grow, you hire.

**4. Simpler banking**
Mainland DET-licensed companies typically experience the smoothest corporate account opening process with UAE's major banks. The combination of a DET license, Ejari, and MOA is a clean, familiar package that banks understand well.

**5. Broader activity scope**
DET allows a wide spectrum of commercial, professional, and industrial activities. A General Trading license under DET lets you import, export, wholesale, and retail a broad range of goods — a flexibility not always available in sector-specific freezones.

**6. Dual presence without complexity**
If you have a freezone company but need UAE mainland access, adding a mainland branch or subsidiary adds cost and complexity. Starting mainland avoids this entirely.

### Who Should Choose Mainland

- Restaurants, cafes, retail shops, and any business requiring a physical customer-facing location
- Service businesses whose clients are primarily UAE-based (clinics, salons, repair services, agencies)
- Companies wanting to bid for government contracts
- Construction and contracting companies
- Real estate brokerages (requires RERA approval)
- Healthcare providers (requires DHA approval)
- Education businesses (requires KHDA approval)
- Any company that expects the majority of its revenue to come from UAE-based clients
- Businesses building a large local team that will need significant visa allocation

### Who Should NOT Choose Mainland

- Companies primarily serving international clients (a freezone is usually more cost-effective)
- Online or digital businesses with no physical UAE customer interaction
- Budget-sensitive founders — the mandatory physical office adds meaningful cost
- Companies wanting to minimize corporate tax (freezone QFZP route may offer 0% on qualifying income)
- Solo founders testing the market (a freezone package is leaner to start)

### The Corporate Tax Reality
Mainland companies pay 9% corporate tax on profits above AED 375,000. There's no pathway to 0% through the QFZP regime — that's only available to freezone companies. However, the Small Business Relief provision means companies with revenue under AED 3 million can elect to pay 0% until December 2026, and profits under AED 375,000 are always exempt.

For a growing mainland business with UAE clients, the 9% is the cost of unrestricted market access. Many businesses find this is a worthwhile trade — especially when you factor in what government contracts can add to revenue.

2. Activity selection (commercial, professional, or industrial license)
3. Initial approval from DET
4. Office lease and Ejari registration

## PAGE 2: FREEZONE OVERVIEW
**URL:** /business-setup/freezone
**Tagline:** 100% Ownership. Tax-Efficient. Set Up in Days.

### What Is a UAE Freezone?
A freezone is a designated economic area that operates under its own independent regulatory authority — separate from the mainland licensing system. The UAE has 45+ freezones across all seven emirates. Each freezone issues its own licenses, sets its own fees, and has its own activity list.

Freezones were created to attract foreign investment by offering something the mainland historically didn't: 100% foreign ownership. Today, the mainland has largely caught up on ownership rules — but freezones still maintain important advantages in cost, speed, tax, and flexibility.

### What Makes Freezones Different
Every UAE freezone shares these baseline advantages:
- **100% foreign ownership** — no local partner or sponsor needed
- **0% personal income tax** — your salary and dividends are tax-free
- **100% profit repatriation** — take all profits out of the UAE freely
- **Potential 0% corporate tax** — via the QFZP (Qualifying Free Zone Person) regime
- **No import/export duties within the freezone** — for goods-based businesses
- **Streamlined digital setup** — most freezones allow complete remote registration
- **Flexible office options** — flexi-desks and virtual offices accepted (unlike mainland)

### The QFZP Tax Advantage
The UAE introduced 9% corporate tax in 2023. But freezone companies can access a 0% rate on qualifying income if they meet the QFZP conditions:
- Registered in a UAE freezone
- Earn "qualifying income" (international services, trading with other freezones, etc.)
- Maintain adequate economic substance in the UAE
- Non-qualifying income (selling to UAE mainland) stays below 5% of total income

For businesses primarily serving international clients or other freezone companies, this is a meaningful advantage over mainland.

### Freezone vs Mainland — The Core Decision
The question isn't "which is better" — it's "where are your customers?"

**Choose Freezone if:**
- Your clients are international (outside UAE)
- Your clients are other freezone companies
- You run an online or digital business
- You're starting lean and want flexible office options
- You want to minimize corporate tax
- You want a faster, simpler setup process

**Choose Mainland if:**
- Your customers are UAE consumers or mainland businesses
- You want to bid on government contracts
- You need a physical customer-facing location (shop, clinic, restaurant)
- You need unlimited visa quota for a large local team

### Not All Freezones Are Equal
This is where most founders get it wrong. "Freezone" is not one product — it's 45+ different options with meaningfully different trade-offs:

**By location credibility:**
Dubai-based freezones (IFZA, Meydan, DMCC, JAFZA) carry more weight with UAE banks and international counterparties than Ajman, Sharjah, or RAK-based freezones. The banking difference is real.

**By activity specialization:**
Some freezones are generalist (IFZA, Meydan) — good for almost any business. Others are specialist — JAFZA for logistics and manufacturing, DMCC for commodities and crypto, ADGM for financial services. Choosing the wrong specialization creates unnecessary friction.

**By cost:**
Budget options (ANC FZ, Shams, RAKEZ) have lower license fees but may trade off on banking access and address credibility. Premium options (DMCC, JAFZA) cost more but deliver ecosystem value and stronger banking relationships.

**By physical infrastructure:**
Most freezones offer only flexi-desks and virtual offices. RAKEZ and JAFZA offer warehouses, industrial plots, and manufacturing facilities.

## PAGE 3: OFFSHORE
**URL:** /business-setup/offshore
**Tagline:** International Holding. Asset Protection. Zero UAE Presence Required.

### What Is a UAE Offshore Company?
A UAE offshore company is a legal entity incorporated in an approved UAE jurisdiction, primarily designed for international business — not for operating within the UAE market.

Unlike mainland and freezone companies, an offshore company:
- Does NOT need a physical office in the UAE
- Does NOT provide UAE residency visas
- CANNOT trade directly within the UAE domestic market
- CAN hold international assets, shares, IP, and investments
- CAN invoice international clients
- CAN open UAE bank accounts for international transactions

Think of it as a UAE-registered holding shell — clean, cost-effective, internationally recognized, with the credibility of a UAE jurisdiction without the operational overhead of running a UAE business.

### Two Offshore Jurisdictions in UAE

**RAK ICC — Ras Al Khaimah International Corporate Centre**
The most popular and fastest-growing offshore registry in the UAE. Over 40,000 incorporations to date. Setup takes 3-7 business days with fully digital processing. No physical visit to RAK required (a registered agent manages the process). Cost-effective. Ideal for holding companies, IP ownership, international trading, family wealth structures, and investment vehicles.

**JAFZA Offshore — Jebel Ali Free Zone Authority**
Dubai's offshore jurisdiction. More expensive and slower than RAK ICC, but carries a "Dubai" address for prestige and banking purposes. The only UAE offshore jurisdiction that allows companies to own freehold property directly in approved Dubai developments (subject to DLD approval). Preferred by larger corporates, international groups needing strong banking relationships, and companies specifically wanting a Dubai-registered address.

For most individual investors and entrepreneurs, RAK ICC is the right choice. For larger structures or Dubai property holding, JAFZA is worth the premium.

### Why Set Up Offshore?

**Asset Protection**
Holding assets in a dedicated legal entity separates them from personal liability. If your operating business faces a dispute or claim, assets held in a separate offshore company are ring-fenced. Common assets: international property, company shares, investment portfolios, trademarks and patents.

**International Holding Structure**
Offshore companies are ideal as the top layer of a corporate structure — owning shares in UAE freezone companies, mainland entities, or foreign companies. This creates a clean ownership chain, simplifies succession, and can optimize dividend flows.

**Intellectual Property Ownership**
Hold trademarks, patents, software, and brand assets in an offshore entity. License them to operating companies. This is a legitimate and widely used structure for IP management and tax efficiency.

**International Invoicing**
Invoice overseas clients from a UAE-registered company without needing UAE residency or a physical office. The UAE's credibility and double-taxation treaty network (100+ countries) make it a respected invoicing jurisdiction.

**Privacy**
UAE offshore jurisdictions limit public access to shareholder and director information, while remaining compliant with international transparency requirements (UBO registers, AML/FATF). Privacy within the law.

**Estate and Succession Planning**
Offshore companies combined with foundations (in ADGM) or trusts provide structured vehicles for multi-generational wealth planning and inheritance management.

### What Offshore Cannot Do
Be clear on limitations before proceeding:
- Cannot conduct business within the UAE domestic market
- Cannot lease commercial office space in the UAE for operations
- Cannot provide UAE investor or employment visas
- Cannot sponsor dependents for UAE residency
- Cannot bid for UAE government contracts
- JAFZA requires at least one visit to UAE (or Power of Attorney); RAK ICC is fully remote

### Compliance in 2026
Offshore companies are not exempt from compliance. Key requirements:
- Annual license renewal
- Ultimate Beneficial Ownership (UBO) declarations (for shareholders owning 25%+)
- Anti-Money Laundering (AML) compliance
- Economic Substance Regulations (ESR) for IP and certain holding activities
- FTA reporting where applicable

We handle ongoing compliance as part of our post-setup support.

### Who Should Choose Offshore?

- International entrepreneurs invoicing global clients who don't need UAE residency
- Investors holding property, shares, or assets internationally
- Founders wanting a holding company above their UAE operating entity
- Businesses managing intellectual property (trademarks, patents, software)
- Family offices structuring wealth across generations
- Companies wanting a UAE-registered entity without the cost of freezone or mainland operations
- Existing business owners restructuring their ownership chain

### Who Should NOT Choose Offshore?

- Founders who need UAE residency — offshore doesn't provide visas
- Businesses with UAE customers — offshore cannot serve the local market
- Companies that need a physical UAE presence
- Anyone who needs to hire UAE-based employees under the company

### The Setup Process
1. Jurisdiction selection (RAK ICC or JAFZA)
2. Company name reservation
3. Document preparation (passport, proof of address, UBO declarations, MOA)
4. Notarization and attestation where required
5. Registered agent appointment (mandatory for both jurisdictions)
6. License issuance (3-7 days RAK ICC, 5-10 days JAFZA)
7. Bank account opening support (Emirates NBD, Mashreq, HSBC, or digital alternatives)
8. Annual compliance setup

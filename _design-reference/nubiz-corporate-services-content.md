### RAK ICC — Ras Al Khaimah International Corporate Centre
**URL:** /business-setup/offshore/rak-icc
**Slug:** rak-icc
**Tagline:** The World's Most Versatile Offshore Registry

**What Is RAK ICC?**
RAK International Corporate Centre is a government-owned offshore registry based in Ras Al Khaimah, UAE. Formed from the consolidation of two earlier IBC registries, RAK ICC has grown into a leading global wealth and corporate structuring centre. With over 40,000 incorporations from 160+ nationalities, it operates at a scale comparable to established offshore centres like the BVI or Cayman Islands — but with the added advantage of UAE jurisdiction, UAE banking access, and a growing recognition by the Dubai Land Department for property ownership.

RAK ICC is the most popular offshore jurisdiction in the UAE by volume, chosen by the majority of entrepreneurs, investors, and holding company structures. It offers the fastest setup time (2-7 business days) and the most cost-effective fee structure of any UAE offshore option.

**Key Features**
- **Fastest setup:** 2-7 business days after document submission — fully digital
- **Fully remote:** No visit to Ras Al Khaimah required; entire process managed by registered agent
- **No minimum share capital:** No capital deposit, no blocked funds
- **Single director structure:** Only one director required (vs two for JAFZA)
- **Privacy:** Shareholder and director details not on public register (UBO disclosure to authorities only)
- **No mandatory audit:** Unlike some jurisdictions, RAK ICC companies are not required to file annual audited accounts (though proper accounting records must be maintained)
- **40,000+ incorporations:** Largest offshore registry in UAE by volume
- **UAE banking access:** Can open multi-currency accounts with Emirates NBD, Mashreq, RAKBANK, and international banks
- **Property ownership:** Can hold real estate in designated freehold areas in Dubai and Ras Al Khaimah (with NOC from RAK ICC registrar)
- **Can own UAE company shares:** Permitted to hold shares in mainland and freezone companies
- **Re-domiciliation:** Existing foreign companies can migrate to RAK ICC without losing corporate history
- **DIFC and ADGM court access:** Contracts and disputes can be governed under English Common Law through these courts
- **100% foreign ownership:** No UAE national partner required

**What RAK ICC Is Used For**
- International holding companies owning shares in other entities
- Asset protection vehicles — separating personal assets from business risk
- Intellectual property holding — trademarks, patents, software
- Family wealth structures and succession planning
- International trading invoicing (between parties outside UAE)
- Investment holding — shares, securities, real estate
- Special Purpose Vehicles (SPVs) for project finance or joint ventures
- Foundation planning alongside ADGM or DIFC structures

**What RAK ICC Cannot Do**
- Trade directly within the UAE domestic market
- Sponsor UAE residency or employment visas for shareholders/directors
- Hire employees in the UAE
- Lease commercial office space for operations
- Bid for UAE government contracts

**The Setup Process**
1. Jurisdiction selection and registered agent appointment (mandatory — cannot apply directly)
2. Company name reservation
3. Document preparation (passport copies, proof of address, source of funds, business purpose)
4. Notarization/attestation where required (varies by country)
5. Submission by registered agent to RAK ICC
6. Certificate of Incorporation issued (2-7 days)
7. Corporate documents package (MOA, share certificates, appointment letters)
8. Bank account opening support

**Annual Compliance**
- Annual license renewal
- Accounting records maintenance (mandatory since 2026)
- UBO declarations (beneficial owners holding 25%+)
- AML compliance
- Economic Substance Regulations (ESR) filing for relevant activities (IP, holding)
- FTA tax registration (required even if 0% liability)

**Who Should Choose RAK ICC**
- Individual investors, entrepreneurs, and SMEs wanting the most cost-effective UAE offshore structure
- Founders needing a holding company above their UAE freezone entity
- Businesses managing international IP, trading, or investments
- Anyone wanting to hold Dubai or RAK real estate under a corporate structure
- Family offices structuring wealth across generations
- Companies re-domiciling from BVI, Cayman, or similar jurisdictions to UAE

**Choose JAFZA Instead If**
- Institutional credibility is paramount (European banks, large investors)
- You specifically need a "Dubai" registered address for contractual reasons
- You are structuring significant real estate investments where JAFZA's DLD relationship offers an advantage
- Your counterparties are conservative banks that favour 40-year established jurisdictions

### JAFZA Offshore — Jebel Ali Free Zone Authority
**URL:** /business-setup/offshore/jafza
**Slug:** jafza-offshore
**Tagline:** Dubai's Most Prestigious Offshore Jurisdiction

**What Is JAFZA Offshore?**
JAFZA Offshore operates under the Jebel Ali Free Zone Authority — one of the world's most recognized freezone brands, established in 1985 and contributing 36% of Dubai's GDP. JAFZA's offshore vehicle has been available since 2003, giving it 20+ years of established banking relationships, institutional recognition, and a track record that most offshore jurisdictions simply cannot match.

JAFZA Offshore is the premium choice for investors, corporate groups, and high-net-worth individuals who need the prestige of a Dubai address, the strongest possible banking relationships, and maximum institutional credibility for their offshore structure.

**Key Features**
- **Dubai address:** Your offshore company is registered within the Jebel Ali Free Zone, Dubai — the UAE's most globally recognized business jurisdiction
- **20+ years of banking relationships:** Emirates NBD, HSBC UAE, Dubai Islamic Bank, Standard Chartered — all have longstanding JAFZA familiarity
- **Dubai property ownership:** Historically JAFZA's exclusive advantage — can hold freehold property in approved Dubai developments under specific DLD conditions
- **Institutional recognition:** Widely recognised by European, Asian, and American financial institutions and investors
- **Strong legal framework:** Operates under JAFZA's comprehensive regulatory framework with 40 years of legal precedent
- **Two-director structure:** Requires minimum two directors (provides governance flexibility for complex structures)
- **100% foreign ownership:** No UAE national partner required
- **Multi-currency banking:** Can hold accounts in AED, USD, EUR, GBP and more
- **Holding company capability:** Can own shares in UAE mainland, freezone, and international companies
- **Setup time:** 7-10 business days (longer than RAK ICC but faster than many jurisdictions)

**Physical Signing Requirement**
JAFZA Offshore generally requires shareholders and directors to sign incorporation documents in person in Dubai, or grant a notarised Power of Attorney to an authorised registered agent. This is a meaningful difference from RAK ICC's fully remote process — factor this in if travel is not feasible.

**What JAFZA Offshore Is Used For**
- Premium holding structures for institutional or large-scale investors
- Dubai real estate holding (the defining use case)
- International corporate groups establishing a UAE presence
- Businesses that deal with European or US institutional investors who specifically recognise JAFZA
- Companies where the "Dubai" address and JAFZA brand adds commercial value to contracts and banking relationships
- Multi-asset holding structures combining UAE property, UAE company shares, and international investments

**What JAFZA Offshore Cannot Do**
- Trade directly within the UAE domestic market
- Sponsor UAE residency or employment visas
- Hire employees in the UAE
- Lease commercial office space for operations

**The Setup Process**
1. Jurisdiction selection and registered agent appointment
2. Company name reservation
3. Document preparation (passport copies, proof of address, source of funds, business plan)
4. Physical signing in Dubai OR notarised Power of Attorney
5. Submission to JAFZA Authority
6. JAFZA review and approval
7. Certificate of Incorporation issued (7-10 days)
8. Corporate documents package
9. Bank account opening support

**Annual Compliance**
- Annual license renewal
- Annual accounts required in some cases (stricter than RAK ICC)
- UBO declarations
- AML compliance
- ESR filings for relevant activities
- FTA tax registration

**Who Should Choose JAFZA Offshore**
- High-net-worth individuals and family offices with significant assets
- Investors requiring the strongest UAE banking relationships
- Companies specifically holding Dubai freehold real estate
- Corporate groups where counterparties recognise and prefer JAFZA
- Structures where the "Dubai" jurisdiction name adds contractual or commercial value
- Investors dealing with European or institutional partners who prefer established jurisdictions with 20+ year track records

**Choose RAK ICC Instead If**
- You want the fastest setup and lowest cost
- You don't need to hold Dubai property specifically
- Your banking needs are standard (Mashreq, RAKBANK, mid-tier UAE banks work fine)
- You want fully remote incorporation with no travel or POA

### PAGE 1: PRO SERVICES
**URL:** /corporate-services/pro-services
**Tagline:** Your Company's Government Liaison — Handled

**What Is PRO?**
PRO stands for Public Relations Officer — the designated person who acts as the bridge between your company and every UAE government authority you need to interact with. Every registered business in the UAE must interact with MOHRE (Ministry of Human Resources), GDRFA (General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs), ICP (Federal Authority for Identity and Citizenship), DET (Department of Economy and Tourism), and freezone authorities. These departments don't automatically talk to each other — someone has to coordinate between them, submit the right documents in the right sequence, and follow up to keep things moving.

That's your PRO. And when you work with NUBIZ, that's us.

**What We Handle**

*License & Company Administration*
- Trade license renewal — annual renewal with DET, freezone authority, or MOHRE before expiry (late renewal triggers AED 250/month fines plus potential penalties)
- Activity amendments — adding or changing business activities on your license
- Trade name updates and amendments
- MOA/AOA amendments — partner changes, share transfers, capital restructuring
- Establishment card (mandatory for hiring) — issuance and renewal
- Branch opening and additional location registration
- Company closure and deregistration procedures

*Document Services*
- Certificate attestation — educational, commercial, and personal documents through MOFA, UAE Embassy, and notary chains
- Document translation — certified Arabic and English translations for official use
- Power of Attorney drafting and attestation

*Government Portal Management*
- MOHRE portal submissions (labour contracts, work permits, WPS compliance)
- GDRFA and ICP applications
- Freezone authority portal submissions (IFZA, Meydan, DMCC and others)
- Medical typing centre coordination for visa applications

- Medical fitness test scheduling and follow-up

*Compliance Deadlines*
We track every renewal deadline across your company's paperwork — license, visas, Emirates IDs, establishment card — and handle renewals proactively. Missing deadlines costs money. We make sure you never do.

**Why Outsource PRO Services?**

Outsourcing PRO services gives you experienced, connected professionals who know exactly which department handles what, how long each step takes, and how to resolve complications — at a fraction of the in-house cost.

**Who Needs PRO Services?**
Every UAE business with employees. The moment you hire your first staff member, you're generating a chain of government processes — entry permit, work permit, medical fitness test, Emirates ID, labor card. Then renewal. Then another employee. Then family visa. Then license renewal. PRO support is not a luxury for large companies — it's operational infrastructure for any business running in the UAE.

### PAGE 2: RESIDENCY SERVICES
**URL:** /corporate-services/residency
**Tagline:** UAE Residency for You, Your Team, and Your Family

**What We Handle**
Getting a UAE residency visa involves multiple steps across multiple government departments — entry permit, medical fitness test, biometric enrollment, Emirates ID, and visa stamping. We manage the entire process end to end for every visa type.

**Employment Visas**
*For companies hiring staff*

An employment visa is sponsored by your company and ties an employee to their job and employer. It's the most common route for expatriate workers in the UAE.

The process: MOHRE work permit approval → Entry permit (ICP/GDRFA) → Medical fitness test → Emirates ID enrollment → Visa stamping. Total time: typically 2-4 weeks from document submission.

What triggers a new employment visa: every new hire. What we handle: the complete MOHRE and ICP/GDRFA process, document preparation, medical coordination, Emirates ID application, and visa stamping follow-up.

Visa renewals: standard employment visas are valid 2 years. We handle renewals proactively before expiry — missed renewal triggers overstay fines of AED 200/day.

Visa cancellations: when an employee leaves, their visa and any family visas linked to them must be cancelled correctly. We manage the full cancellation process and final settlement coordination.

*Key points:*
- Employer covers all visa costs by UAE law — costs cannot be passed to the employee
- Each new hire requires: work permit from MOHRE, entry permit, medical fitness test, biometrics, Emirates ID
- We handle the entire process — you hand us documents, we deliver visa

**Family Visas**
*For sponsors bringing dependents to UAE*

UAE residents can sponsor their immediate family — spouse, children, and in some cases parents — provided they meet minimum salary and housing requirements.

What's required: attested marriage certificate, birth certificates for children, tenancy contract or property ownership proof, salary certificate or bank statement, medical fitness for dependents 18+.

Family visa duration matches the sponsor's visa validity. When the sponsor's visa renews, family visas renew simultaneously.

What we handle: document attestation, application submission, medical coordination, Emirates ID applications, and follow-up until all family members have valid residence.

**Investor & Partner Visas**
*For company owners and shareholders*

Business owners and partners in UAE-registered companies qualify for investor or partner visas — allowing them to live in the UAE as the owner of their own company rather than as an employee.

Requirements vary by structure: freezone company owners need their trade license and establishment card; mainland company owners may need the license plus MOA showing ownership; minimum share values may apply depending on emirate and license type.

What we handle: investor visa processing through the relevant freezone authority or mainland immigration channel, medical coordination, Emirates ID, and the full residency stamping process.

**Golden Visa**
*10-year UAE residency — no employer required*

The UAE Golden Visa grants 10-year self-sponsored residency without requiring a UAE employer or sponsor. Categories include: investors (AED 2M+ in property or public investments), entrepreneurs with approved projects, scientists, outstanding students, and senior professionals in specific sectors.

Benefits: 10-year renewable residency, can stay outside UAE for extended periods without visa cancellation, full family sponsorship on the same terms, no employer dependency.

What we handle: eligibility assessment, document preparation and attestation, submission through appropriate channels (DLD for property investors, ICP/GDRFA for other categories), follow-up through issuance.

Processing time: 4-8 weeks typically, up to 12 weeks for complex cases.

**Green Visa**
*5-year self-sponsored residency for skilled professionals*

The Green Visa is a middle ground between the 2-year employment visa (employer-tied) and the 10-year Golden Visa (high thresholds). It gives 5-year self-sponsored residency to:
- Skilled employees earning AED 15,000+/month with at least a bachelor's degree
- Freelancers with a valid permit and AED 360,000+ annual income
- Investors in UAE-registered businesses

What we handle: eligibility check, document preparation, ICP submission, medical coordination, Emirates ID, and activation.

**All Residency Includes**
Every visa we process includes coordination of:
- Medical fitness test scheduling at DHA-approved centres
- Emirates ID biometrics and application
- Health insurance coordination (mandatory in Dubai)
- Status change processing if applicant is already in UAE
- Tracking and follow-up through every stage until visa is stamped

### PAGE 3: ACCOUNTING & BOOKKEEPING
**URL:** /corporate-services/accounting
**Tagline:** Compliant Books. Zero Penalties. Real Numbers.

**Why Accounting Matters More in 2026**
Before 2023, many UAE businesses treated accounting as a once-a-year admin task. UAE Corporate Tax changed that permanently. Your tax return is built directly from your accounting records. Your VAT return depends on accurate transaction recording. If your books are wrong, your tax filings are wrong — and FTA penalties range from AED 1,000 for minor errors to AED 50,000 for serious non-compliance.

Under UAE Corporate Tax Law, every business must maintain IFRS-compliant financial records for a minimum of 7 years. This is not optional — it's a legal requirement.

Small Business Relief (0% tax for businesses under AED 3M revenue) expires December 2026. If your business is approaching this threshold, structured accounting becomes urgent — not just good practice.

**What We Handle**

*Monthly Bookkeeping*
Recording every transaction — sales, expenses, bank deposits, supplier payments — correctly categorized and reconciled against your bank statements. This is the foundation everything else builds on. We work on cloud-based platforms (Xero, QuickBooks, Zoho Books) giving you real-time visibility into your finances from anywhere.

*VAT Returns*
If your taxable supplies exceed AED 375,000 annually, VAT registration is mandatory. We handle: VAT registration with the FTA, quarterly VAT return preparation and EmaraTax submission, and year-round input tax optimization to ensure you claim everything you're entitled to. Late VAT filing: AED 1,000 first offence, AED 2,000 repeat — plus 2% late payment penalty immediately, escalating to 1%/day uncapped.

*Corporate Tax*
Since June 2023, UAE companies pay 9% on taxable profits above AED 375,000. Freezone companies can qualify for 0% through QFZP status — but only if they meet specific FTA conditions around qualifying income, economic substance, and business activities. We assess your eligibility, structure your compliance to maximize any available relief, file your annual Corporate Tax return (due within 9 months of year-end), and maintain the records FTA requires for audit readiness.

*Financial Statements*
IFRS-compliant profit & loss, balance sheet, and cash flow statements prepared monthly and annually. Required for: trade license renewal (in many freezones), bank account maintenance, investor reporting, FTA audit readiness, and loan applications.

*Payroll & WPS*
Monthly payroll processing including salary calculations, leave accruals, end-of-service gratuity calculations, and Wages Protection System (WPS) submission — mandatory for mainland UAE companies. We handle the full payroll cycle and ensure every payment goes through the system correctly, with the right records maintained for MOHRE compliance.

*Audit Preparation*
Some freezone companies are required by their freezone authority to submit audited financial statements annually. We prepare audit-ready books and coordinate with FTA-registered approved auditors to complete the process with minimal disruption to your business. Audit fees are separate from accounting fees.

**Why Outsource Accounting?**
In-house accountant in Dubai: AED 10,000–20,000/month in full employment costs. Outsourced accounting package covering all the above: AED 1,500–5,000/month for most SMEs. The accounting firm's fees are also fully deductible as a business expense for Corporate Tax purposes — reducing your net cost further.

**Our Accounting Service**
One named accountant assigned to your file. Monthly reports delivered within the first week of the following month. You always have access to your own financial data through your cloud accounting platform. We file every return before the deadline — you never pay an FTA late-filing penalty.

### PAGE 4: BANK ACCOUNT OPENING
**URL:** /corporate-services/bank-account
**Tagline:** Your UAE Bank Account, Done Right the First Time

**The Reality of UAE Corporate Banking**
UAE banks are some of the most compliant and stringent in the world. This is a feature, not a bug — it makes UAE banking internationally credible and trusted. But for new businesses, it means the account opening process requires careful preparation. A poorly presented application is the #1 reason for delays and rejections — not a problematic business model.

UAE banks apply rigorous KYC (Know Your Customer) and AML (Anti-Money Laundering) procedures. Every application is assessed on: clarity of business model, source of funds, ownership structure, expected transaction patterns, and the risk profile of the business activity.

The good news: with proper preparation and the right bank match for your business type, most UAE company bank accounts can be opened within 20-40 working days.

**What We Do**

*Bank Selection*
Not every bank is right for every business. We match your company's profile to the bank most likely to approve your application — based on your business activity, ownership structure, nationality, expected transaction volume, and whether you're a resident or non-resident. Using the wrong bank wastes weeks.

Major UAE banks we work with: Emirates NBD, Mashreq, ADCB, FAB, RAKBank, Dubai Islamic Bank, HSBC UAE, Standard Chartered, Wio (digital), and others depending on profile and need.

*Document Preparation*
We prepare and review your complete KYC package before submission — ensuring every document is current, consistent, and presented in the format each bank expects. Common package: trade license, MOA, establishment card, passport and Emirates ID copies of shareholders, proof of address, business profile, expected transaction explanation, source of funds declaration, and supporting business evidence (contracts, invoices, supplier agreements).

*Business Profile*
One of the most common rejection reasons is a vague or inconsistent business description. We help you articulate your business model clearly and compellingly for bank compliance teams — in plain, direct language that answers their questions before they ask them.

*Bank Introductions & Meetings*
We coordinate appointments and attend bank meetings alongside you where appropriate. Our established relationships with bank relationship managers at major UAE institutions mean your application gets reviewed by someone who understands your business type, not just processed through a generic compliance queue.

*Application Tracking*
We track every stage of your application and follow up proactively with the bank's compliance team to prevent stalls. If a bank requests additional documents, we turn them around fast. If an application is rejected, we diagnose why and reapply with a different banking partner — at no additional consultation fee.

**For Different Business Types**

*Consultants and service businesses:* digital banks (Wio) or SME-focused branches at Mashreq or RAKBank are often the fastest route to approval with straightforward documentation.

*Trading companies (import/export):* Emirates NBD and FAB have stronger trade finance desks; be prepared with supplier information, expected cargo types, and end-buyer profiles.

*Freezone companies:* all major banks accept freezone-licensed businesses; the freezone authority's reputation affects initial KYC friction. DMCC, IFZA, and Meydan companies generally have the smoothest experience.

*Non-resident shareholders:* expect extended due diligence and stronger source-of-funds requirements. At least one UAE resident as an authorised signatory significantly speeds the process.

*Offshore companies (RAK ICC / JAFZA):* more complex; JAFZA companies have historical banking relationships; RAK ICC works well with Mashreq, RAKBANK, and international banks. We assess your profile before recommending.

**After Opening**
We support with: multi-currency account activation, online banking setup and user roles, cheque book ordering, and ongoing banking queries for the first 90 days.

**Key Facts**
- Most UAE corporate accounts open in 20-40 working days
- Minimum balance requirements typically AED 25,000–100,000 depending on bank and account type
- At least one UAE resident signatory significantly improves approval speed
- We do not guarantee bank approval — banks make the final decision — but our preparation process substantially improves the outcome
